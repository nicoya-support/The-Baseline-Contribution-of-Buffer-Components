How to use

  • Click the + or - buttons to add or remove components, respectively.
  • Make a selection from the components drop-down menu to choose a new component.
  • Use the slider or enter a value to change a component concentration.
    Use the up/down controls of the input field or the arrow keys to adjust a concentration more precisely.
  • Make a selection from the unit drop-down menu to change the concentration unit.
    Have the 'Convert concentration when changing unit' checkbox ticked to automatically convert concentrations accordingly.
  • Choose a different optical path length from the path length drop-down menu, if desired.
  • Adjust the target absorbance cut-off value in the corresponding input field, if desired.

Spectral regions where absorbance will be larger than the target absorbance cut-off are calculated based on all chosen components and settings and are reported in orange.

Frequently asked questions

The data presented are for guidance only; they represent approximations and are not meant to serve as a replacement for blank measurements. Always measure your buffer baseline as usual for accurate and precise data.

Absorbance cut-offs provided by the tool should be reasonable approximations within ±1 nm, but are subject to certain factors of uncertainty. For example, when comparing with your own data, some variance may occur depending on the state of your spectrometer, including calibration and how well the system has been purged with nitrogen.

Moreover, please note that the tool assumes additivity of the chosen components, i.e.:

  • Potential spectral changes due to complex formation are not taken into account (e.g., selection of both MgCl2 and EDTA, or selection of both surfactants and cyclodextrin).
  • Limitations regarding solubility or miscibility are not taken into account; i.e., chosen combinations / concentrations might not be physically possible.
  • Likewise, other potential interactions between components and their effect on absorbance are not taken into account.

Note that there are some deliberate constraints, e.g.:

  • The first component entry only allows selection of solvents to ensure that always at least one solvent is included; the concentration of this reference solvent will be adjusted if more solvents are added.
  • The input absorbance is limited to 2 ± 0.5 A.U. because accuracy of approximations is limited at lower and higher absorbance values.
  • Concentration ranges are either limited to maximum compound solubility (if known) or to arbitrary upper limits that are considered reasonable for typical applications.

Please note that, for components that allow selection of both molar (e.g., mM) and mass concentrations (e.g., mg/mL), mass concentrations refer to the anhydrous compound.

The absorbance shown is never zero—even at higher wavelengths—but always has a small offset of up to ~0.06 AU. This is due to a spectral contribution by the sample cuvette / cell itself.

The spectral contribution of most buffers and excipients to CD or absorbance beyond the far-UV range is usually negligible and, therefore, higher wavelengths have been omitted.

Some components might have deliberately been excluded from the list; for example, sodium fluoride (NaF) is being excluded because its absorbance is negligible even at low molar concentrations.

Our capacities for expanding the list of components are limited in terms of both time and materials. However, if you don't find a specific component that you are looking for in the list, you can help us expanding it by sending us a sample of your component. Please contact us!
